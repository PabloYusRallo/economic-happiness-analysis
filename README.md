# Economic Analysis of Happiness 🌍📊

## Overview
This project explores the relationship between economic indicators and happiness levels across countries using the World Happiness dataset.

## Research Question
Is GDP per capita the main determinant of happiness, or do social and institutional factors also play an important role?

## Dataset
World Happiness Report dataset including variables such as GDP per capita, social support, life expectancy, freedom, and perceptions of corruption.

## Tools Used
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib

## Key Findings
- GDP per capita shows a strong positive correlation with happiness (~0.79), making it the most influential single economic variable.
- Social support and life expectancy also exhibit significant positive relationships with happiness.
- Overall, happiness appears to be a multidimensional concept influenced by both economic and non-economic factors.

## Conclusion
While economic prosperity is a key driver of happiness, it is not sufficient on its own to explain differences in well-being across countries. Social and institutional factors also play an important complementary role.
